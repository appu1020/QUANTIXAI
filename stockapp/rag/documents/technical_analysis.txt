Financial Technical Analysis Knowledge Base
=========================================

This document contains definitions and interpretations of technical indicators
used by the QuantixAI platform. It serves as a primary knowledge source for
the RAG-powered AI Financial Assistant.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TREND INDICATORS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SIMPLE MOVING AVERAGE (SMA)
The Simple Moving Average is calculated by averaging the closing prices over
a specified number of periods. SMA20 represents a short-term trend while
SMA50 and SMA200 represent medium and long-term trends respectively.

Interpretation:
- When price is above SMA200, the long-term trend is bullish (uptrend).
- When price crosses above SMA50, a medium-term buy signal is generated.
- The "Golden Cross" occurs when SMA50 crosses above SMA200 — a strong bullish signal.
- The "Death Cross" occurs when SMA50 crosses below SMA200 — a strong bearish signal.
- Price bouncing off SMA20 in an uptrend indicates continuation.

EXPONENTIAL MOVING AVERAGE (EMA)
EMA gives more weight to recent prices compared to SMA, making it more
responsive to new information. EMA20 and EMA50 are commonly used.

Interpretation:
- EMA crossovers generate buy/sell signals similar to SMA but more quickly.
- Price above EMA20 suggests short-term bullish momentum.
- EMA20 crossing above EMA50 is a bullish signal; crossing below is bearish.
- EMA is preferred over SMA when market conditions are rapidly changing.

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
MACD consists of: MACD Line (EMA12 - EMA26), Signal Line (EMA9 of MACD),
and Histogram (MACD Line - Signal Line).

Interpretation:
- Bullish Signal: MACD crosses above the signal line; histogram turns positive.
- Bearish Signal: MACD crosses below the signal line; histogram turns negative.
- Bullish Divergence: Price makes lower lows but MACD makes higher lows — reversal signal.
- Bearish Divergence: Price makes higher highs but MACD makes lower highs — reversal signal.
- Strong momentum: MACD histogram bars growing in size.

AVERAGE DIRECTIONAL INDEX (ADX)
ADX measures trend strength regardless of direction. Range: 0–100.

Interpretation:
- ADX < 20: Weak trend or ranging market — avoid trend-following strategies.
- ADX 20–25: Trend emerging — monitor for confirmation.
- ADX 25–50: Strong trend — trend-following strategies are appropriate.
- ADX > 50: Very strong trend — trend is likely to continue.
- ADX rising: Trend is strengthening.
- ADX falling: Trend is weakening.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MOMENTUM INDICATORS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

RELATIVE STRENGTH INDEX (RSI)
RSI measures the magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or
oversold conditions. Developed by J. Welles Wilder. Range: 0–100.

Interpretation:
- RSI > 70: Overbought — price may be due for a pullback or correction.
- RSI < 30: Oversold — price may be due for a bounce or reversal.
- RSI = 50: Neutral momentum.
- RSI crossing 50 upward: Bullish momentum signal.
- RSI crossing 50 downward: Bearish momentum signal.
- Bullish divergence (price drops, RSI rises): Potential reversal upward.
- Bearish divergence (price rises, RSI drops): Potential reversal downward.

STOCHASTIC RSI
Stochastic RSI applies the Stochastic oscillator to RSI values.
It consists of %K and %D lines. Range: 0–100.

Interpretation:
- StochRSI > 80: Overbought — consider taking profits or tightening stops.
- StochRSI < 20: Oversold — potential buying opportunity.
- %K crossing above %D: Bullish signal.
- %K crossing below %D: Bearish signal.
- More sensitive than RSI — better for short-term trades.

RATE OF CHANGE (ROC)
ROC measures percentage change in price over a specified period.

Interpretation:
- Positive ROC: Price is higher than it was N periods ago — bullish momentum.
- Negative ROC: Price is lower — bearish momentum.
- ROC crossing zero upward: Momentum turning bullish.
- ROC crossing zero downward: Momentum turning bearish.
- Extreme ROC values suggest potential mean reversion.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VOLATILITY INDICATORS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

BOLLINGER BANDS
Bollinger Bands consist of a middle band (SMA20) and upper/lower bands
set at 2 standard deviations from the middle.

Interpretation:
- Price touching upper band: Potentially overbought — watch for reversal.
- Price touching lower band: Potentially oversold — watch for bounce.
- Band squeeze (bands narrowing): Low volatility — breakout likely soon.
- Band expansion: Increased volatility and strong trend.
- Price walking up the upper band: Strong uptrend, not a sell signal alone.
- Price walking down the lower band: Strong downtrend.
- W-bottom pattern (two lows near lower band): Bullish reversal.
- M-top pattern (two highs near upper band): Bearish reversal.

AVERAGE TRUE RANGE (ATR)
ATR measures market volatility by calculating the average range of price
movement including gaps.

Interpretation:
- High ATR: High volatility — use wider stop losses.
- Low ATR: Low volatility — price range is narrow.
- ATR rising: Volatility increasing — potential for large moves.
- ATR falling: Volatility decreasing — market consolidating.
- ATR is used for position sizing: smaller positions in high ATR environments.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VOLUME INDICATORS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

VOLUME WEIGHTED AVERAGE PRICE (VWAP)
VWAP represents the average price weighted by volume, widely used by
institutional traders as a benchmark.

Interpretation:
- Price above VWAP: Bullish bias — institutional buyers are in control.
- Price below VWAP: Bearish bias — selling pressure is dominant.
- VWAP as support: Price pulling back to VWAP in an uptrend may bounce.
- VWAP as resistance: Price rallying to VWAP in a downtrend may reverse.
- Institutional traders often use VWAP to benchmark execution quality.

ON-BALANCE VOLUME (OBV)
OBV adds volume on up days and subtracts it on down days, creating a
cumulative volume-price momentum indicator.

Interpretation:
- Rising OBV with rising price: Healthy uptrend confirmed by volume.
- Falling OBV with falling price: Downtrend confirmed by volume.
- OBV rising but price flat: Accumulation — possible bullish breakout soon.
- OBV falling but price flat: Distribution — possible bearish breakdown.
- OBV divergence from price: Early warning of potential trend reversal.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PRICE ACTION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SUPPORT AND RESISTANCE
Support is a price level where buying interest is strong enough to prevent
further decline. Resistance is where selling pressure prevents further rise.

Interpretation:
- Price approaching support: Potential buying zone (long opportunity).
- Price approaching resistance: Potential selling zone (short opportunity).
- Breakout above resistance: Resistance becomes support — bullish signal.
- Breakdown below support: Support becomes resistance — bearish signal.
- Strong support/resistance: Multiple touches without breaking = stronger level.

CANDLESTICK PATTERNS

Bullish Engulfing:
A large bullish candle completely engulfs the previous bearish candle.
Signal: Strong reversal from downtrend — bullish. Reliability increases near support.

Bearish Engulfing:
A large bearish candle completely engulfs the previous bullish candle.
Signal: Strong reversal from uptrend — bearish. Reliability increases near resistance.

Hammer:
Small body at the top, long lower shadow (≥ 2× body), tiny upper shadow.
Signal: Bullish reversal, especially at support. Indicates buyers defended low prices.

Shooting Star:
Small body at the bottom, long upper shadow (≥ 2× body), tiny lower shadow.
Signal: Bearish reversal, especially at resistance. Indicates sellers pushed price back.

Doji:
Open and close are nearly equal, creating a cross shape.
Signal: Indecision — potential reversal, especially after a strong trend.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BREAKOUT SIGNALS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Breakout Up:
When price closes above the most recent 20-period resistance level.
Signal: Potential new uptrend beginning. Volume confirmation strengthens the signal.
Risk: False breakouts are common — look for 1-2 closes above resistance.

Breakout Down:
When price closes below the most recent 20-period support level.
Signal: Potential new downtrend beginning. High volume increases reliability.
Risk: Dead-cat bounces can trap shorts — wait for confirmation.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TREND STRENGTH COMPOSITE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TREND STRENGTH (EMA12 - EMA26)
A simplified momentum measure derived from the difference between short and
long exponential moving averages.

Interpretation:
- Positive value: Short-term EMA above long-term EMA — bullish momentum.
- Negative value: Short-term EMA below long-term EMA — bearish momentum.
- Increasing magnitude: Trend strengthening.
- Decreasing magnitude: Trend weakening, possible consolidation.
- Zero crossing: Potential trend change.
